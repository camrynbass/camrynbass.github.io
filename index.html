Lung cancer and small cell lung carcinoma

Lung cancer is split into two major categories: small cell lung carcinoma (SCLC) and non-small cell lung carcinoma (NSCLC).

SCLC is generally considered more aggressive than NSCLC, and more difficult to treat clinically. Most patients are diagnosed with SCLC when it is already at an advanced stage. In Canada, the median overall survival (OS) of all SCLC patients after diagnosis is 9.5 months. Patients with advanced SCLC have a median OS of 6.5 months, and patients with early-stage SCLC have a median OS of 19.8 months.

These poor survival rates persist despite years of research on this topic
